# Interview Talking Points: Apple Financial Analysis & 3-Year Forecast

## 1. How I Would Explain This Project

I built this project to demonstrate end-to-end financial analysis, forecasting, and executive reporting skills using public Apple financial statement data.

The project includes historical financial analysis, ratio analysis, free cash flow analysis, a 3-year scenario forecast, Python-based automation, an Excel financial model, and dashboard planning.

## 2. Business Problem

The goal was to evaluate Apple’s financial health and understand how revenue, profitability, cash flow, and balance sheet strength have changed over time.

I also wanted to create a forward-looking forecast to show how different business assumptions can impact revenue, net income, and free cash flow.

## 3. Data Used

I used public Apple financial statement data from annual reports and structured the data into clean datasets for analysis.

The main financial metrics included:

- Revenue
- Gross profit
- Operating income
- Net income
- Total assets
- Total liabilities
- Shareholders' equity
- Operating cash flow
- Capital expenditures
- Free cash flow
- Diluted EPS

## 4. Key Metrics Calculated

I calculated several financial ratios and KPIs, including:

- Revenue growth %
- Gross margin %
- Operating margin %
- Net margin %
- Current ratio
- Debt-to-equity ratio
- Return on assets
- Return on equity
- Free cash flow margin %
- CapEx as % of revenue

## 5. Forecasting Approach

I built a 3-year forecast model for FY2026 through FY2028 using three scenarios:

- Base Case
- Upside Case
- Downside Case

The forecast uses assumptions for revenue growth, gross margin, operating margin, net margin, capital expenditures, and free cash flow margin.

## 6. Key Findings

Apple showed strong revenue growth from FY2021 to FY2025, with revenue increasing from approximately $365.8B to $416.2B.

Profitability remained strong, and gross margin improved over the period.

Free cash flow remained consistently high, showing Apple’s ability to generate cash and support shareholder returns, reinvestment, and long-term financial flexibility.
